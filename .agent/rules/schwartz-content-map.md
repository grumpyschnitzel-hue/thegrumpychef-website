# Schwartz Content Map — Pain Points × Awareness Stages

> **Purpose:** Maps every pain point to every awareness stage. Given a pain point and a stage, this file tells you the angle, the hook direction, and the CTA destination.
> **Source framework:** Eugene Schwartz's 5 Levels of Market Awareness, adapted for restaurant operators.

---

## The 5 Awareness Stages

| Stage | What They Know | What They Need | Content Goal |
|-------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| **1. Unaware** | Don't know they have the problem | Wake-up call | Make the problem visible |
| **2. Problem Aware** | Know something's wrong, don't know what | Diagnosis | Name the pain precisely |
| **3. Solution Aware** | Know solutions exist, haven't picked one | Comparison / framework | Show YOUR approach is different |
| **4. Product Aware** | Know about The Grumpy Chef, evaluating | Proof + specifics | Remove final objections |
| **5. Most Aware** | Ready to buy, just need the push | Clear offer + urgency | Make the next step frictionless |

## Stage-Based Content Rules

| **Unaware** | 40% of content | LinkedIn posts, Reels, Substack Notes | Cold — question only, no offer |

## How to Use This Map

1. **Pick a pain point** from the 8 above
2. **Choose the awareness stage** that matches your audience for today's content
3. **Read the angle and hook direction** — this is your content brief
4. **Pull a hook from `hook-library.md`** that matches the pain point × stage
5. **Pull a CTA from `cta-library.md`** that matches the destination
6. **Write using the formula in `SKILL.md`:** failure → lie → turn → lesson → proof → offer
7. **Format using `platform-templates.md`** for the target platform
