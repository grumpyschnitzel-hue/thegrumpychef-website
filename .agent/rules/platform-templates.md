# Platform Format Templates

> **Purpose:** Structural containers for each platform. Character limits, line breaks, hook placement, CTA positioning.
> **Not in this file:** Writing formula, voice rules, pain points, hooks, CTAs. Those live in their own files.
> **Rule:** SKILL.md applies the writing formula, then pours it into the platform template below.

---

## The Governing Principle: Trust Before Conversion

**Every piece of content must pass this test before it goes out.**

Do not aim to sell on the first touch. Aim to earn trust.

The audience scrolls with their guard up. The second your content starts sounding like it's headed toward a pitch, you become background noise. People don't leave because the offer is bad — they leave because they can feel the sell coming.

**The job of every post is to give the viewer a win right now:**
- Make them understand something in 20 seconds
- Make them feel seen
- Make their problem feel smaller
- Make the solution feel doable

If your content does not improve their day in some way, it is asking for attention instead of deserving it.

**The proof problem:** If all the value lives behind your paid products, your content has no proof. You don't need to give away the whole system. But you do need to show enough for someone to think: *"That was actually helpful. I want more from this person."*

**The best marketing feels like help.** Trust is what makes someone stay. Trust is what makes someone come back. Trust is what makes someone click when you finally do invite them deeper.

This principle overrides everything below. If a template, CTA, or format pushes toward conversion at the expense of trust — the template is wrong, not the principle.

## 1. LinkedIn Text Post

### Format Specs
- **Character limit:** 3,000 max
- **Sweet spot:** 800–1,600 characters (150–300 words)
- **"See more" cut:** ~140–210 characters — first 1–3 lines must carry the hook
- **Paragraphs:** 1–2 sentences each, heavy white space, left-aligned
- **Emojis:** Minimal to none (brand rule)
- **Hashtags:** 2–5 max, placed at the end

### Structural Template
```
HOOK (lines 1–3, before "see more" fold)
[Contrarian, ultra-specific, or result-based opening]

CONTEXT / PROBLEM (2–3 short paragraphs)
[Describe the situation in their words]

INSIGHT / SOLUTION (3–6 short bullets or mini-paragraphs)
[Concrete points, steps, or framework]

PROOF / EXAMPLE (1–2 paragraphs, optional)
[One specific story or micro-case study]

CTA (final 1–2 lines)
[Single clear action — question, comment trigger, or DM keyword]
```

### Formula Adaptation
The full 7-step writing formula fits naturally in this format. Hook = failure/vulnerability. Context = the lie. Insight = the turn + lesson. Proof = proof. CTA = offer. Compress or expand based on target length.

### What Works
- AIDA and PAS frameworks convert best here
- Posts under 800 chars feel thin; over 1,600 chars lose casual scrollers
- Line breaks between every 1–2 sentences (mobile readability)
- One CTA only — split focus kills engagement

## 2. LinkedIn Carousel

### Format Specs
- **Format:** Multi-page PDF uploaded as document post
- **Slide count:** 5–10 slides (sweet spot for engagement)
- **Text per slide:** 25–50 words, 2–4 short lines, one core idea per slide
- **Design:** Large headline, readable fonts, white space, max 3 colors
- **Dimensions:** 1080×1080 or 1080×1350 per page

### Structural Template (10-Slide Standard)
```
SLIDE 1 — COVER/HOOK
[6–10 word title with clear promise or pain]
[Optional subhead: who it's for]

SLIDE 2 — RE-HOOK / CONTEXT
[1 line: core problem or situation]
[2–3 bullets: what this carousel delivers]

SLIDES 3–7 — BODY (one idea per slide)
[Mini-headline per slide]
[2–4 short lines or bullets with concrete advice]

SLIDE 8 — SUMMARY / RECAP
[One sentence recap]
[Bullet list of key points from slides 3–7]

SLIDE 9 — CONVERSATION QUESTION
[One question to trigger comments]

SLIDE 10 — CTA (one action only)
[Save, DM keyword, or follow]
```

### Three Carousel Structures

**A) How-To / Step-by-Step**
- Cover: "How to [RESULT] without [PAIN]"
- Slide 2: "Here's the X-step system"
- Slides 3–7: One step per slide
- Slide 8: Recap steps
- Slide 9: "Which step are you skipping?"
- Slide 10: CTA

**B) Problem → Mistakes → Solution**
- Cover: Call out painful problem
- Slide 2: Short story/context
- Slides 3–4: Common mistakes (one per slide)
- Slides 5–7: Framework/solution (one pillar per slide)
- Slide 8: Recap
- Slide 9: "Which mistake hits home?"
- Slide 10: CTA

**C) Checklist / Framework**
- Cover: "The X-point checklist for [RESULT]"
- Slide 2: Why this checklist matters
- Slides 3–9: One checklist item per slide
- Slide 10: CTA

### Formula Adaptation
One formula step per slide. Cover slide = hook/failure. Slides 3–7 spread the turn + lesson across individual frames. Final slide = offer. Compress the 7-step formula into the slide count available.

### What Works
- Carousels drive high dwell time (swipe = engagement signal)
- Mobile-first: if text isn't readable at phone size, it fails
- End slide CTA must be ONE action — not three options

## 3. Substack Notes

### Format Specs
- **Length:** Under 250 words — engagement drops sharply beyond this
- **Frequency:** Daily or near-daily for subscriber growth
- **Tone:** Like messaging a smart friend — vulnerable, personal, less corporate than LinkedIn
- **Links:** Use sparingly — too many links = billboard, not conversation

### Structural Template
```
HOOK (first sentence — must stop the scroll)
[Contrarian, specific outcome, or intriguing question]

BODY (1–4 short lines or bullets)
[One insight, one mini-story, or one framework point]
[Line breaks between thoughts, 1–2 bullets max]

CTA (conversation-focused)
[Question that invites replies — not "subscribe now"]
```

### Formula Adaptation
Compress the 7-step formula to 2–3 steps max. Pick the strongest element: either the failure + lesson, or the lie + turn. Notes are too short for the full sequence. Save the complete formula for LinkedIn text or newsletter.

### What Works
- "Here's what I'm trying / noticing" beats "10 rules you must follow"
- Questions and genuine conversation outperform promo
- Consistent daily posting > occasional perfect Notes
- Goal: discovery and conversation, not authority flex

### What Doesn't Work
- Long walls of text
- Corporate/authority tone
- Repeated "subscribe to my newsletter" CTAs
- Multiple links in one Note

## 4. Facebook Group Posts

### Format Specs
- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, not authority-to-audience
- **Links:** Minimal — one max, and only after delivering value
- **Engagement:** Algorithm rewards comment threads, replies, back-and-forth
- **Compliance:** Follow group rules strictly — promo gets flagged and removed

### Structural Template
```
CONTEXT LINE
["Quick story from my X years running a busy [type] spot..."]

MICRO-STORY / INSIGHT (3–6 short lines)
[The stuck situation → the tweak → the outcome]

PEER-TO-PEER QUESTION CTA
["Curious — how are you handling [X] in your place?"]

OPTIONAL SOFT PROMO (only if group rules allow, after real value)
["If helpful, I put my checklist in a free doc — happy to DM it."]
```

### Formula Adaptation
Lead with story elements only — failure + turn + lesson. Drop the explicit offer. In groups, the CTA is always a question or a soft DM offer, never a hard sell. The formula's "proof" step works well here as a peer-shared result, not a testimonial.

### What Works
- PAS and Story frameworks perform best
- Native text with genuine questions
- Replies to comments (creates real discussion threads)
- "Fellow operator sharing what worked" framing

### What Gets Flagged / Down-Ranked
- Multiple external links
- Clickbait headlines
- "Like this if you agree" engagement bait
- "Tag 3 friends" or similar spam patterns
- Repeated promo wording or hashtags
- Obviously copied/templated posts

## 5. Instagram

### 5a. Instagram Reels

#### Format Specs
- **Length:** 20–30 seconds sweet spot for completion rate
- **Orientation:** Vertical 9:16, full-screen mobile
- **Resolution:** 1080p minimum, 4K preferred
- **Edit pace:** Cut every 1.5–2 seconds (cuts, zooms, B-roll, text changes)
- **Hook window:** First 1–3 seconds — if they don't stay past 3s, Reel dies
- **Captions:** Burned-in subtitles always (many watch on mute)
- **Text safe zone:** Keep on-screen text clear of UI overlay areas
- **Posting cadence:** 3–5 Reels/week, consistent beats sporadic

#### Structural Template
```
HOOK (0–2 seconds)
[Pattern interrupt — visual or verbal jolt that breaks the scroll]

PROMISE (2–4 seconds)
[What they'll get if they keep watching]

BODY (4–25 seconds)
[Steps, story, demo — fast cuts, one idea per segment]

PAYOFF (25–28 seconds)
[The result, the transformation, the punchline]

CTA (final 2–3 seconds)
[Save, share, follow, comment, or "link in bio"]
```

#### Formula Adaptation
Compress the 7-step formula heavily. Pick 2–3 strongest elements: hook = failure/vulnerability, body = the turn, payoff = the lesson with proof. Drop explicit offer — use "link in bio" or caption CTA instead. The full formula doesn't fit in 30 seconds.

#### What Works
- Before/after transformations (visually obvious changes)
- Fast micro-tutorials (3 tips, 1 hack, 1 mistake to avoid)
- Contrarian takes that spark comments and shares
- Real behind-the-scenes (not polished, not staged)
- Strong 3-second hold determines everything

#### What Doesn't Work
- Long intros before the hook
- Heavy selling within the Reel itself
- Low-quality video or audio
- TikTok watermarks (algorithm deprioritizes)
- Inconsistent posting then silence

### 5b. Instagram Carousel Posts

#### Format Specs
- **Slide count:** 5–7 slides
- **Dimensions:** 1080×1080 or 1080×1350
- **Text per slide:** Scannable, large fonts, one idea per slide
- **Design:** Clean, on-brand, mobile-readable

#### Structural Template
```
SLIDE 1 — HOOK/COVER
[Clear promise or pain statement — stops the scroll]

SLIDES 2–5 — BODY (one idea per slide)
[Break down a playbook, checklist, or system]
[Mini-headline + 2–3 short lines per slide]

SLIDE 6 — RECAP / KEY TAKEAWAY
[One-sentence summary of the value]

SLIDE 7 — CTA
[Save, share, follow, or "DM me [KEYWORD]"]
```

#### Formula Adaptation
Similar to LinkedIn carousel but shorter (5–7 vs 5–10 slides). One formula step per slide. Cover = hook/failure. Body slides = turn + lesson. Final slide = soft offer or save CTA. Instagram carousels skew more visual than LinkedIn — less text per slide.

### 5c. Instagram Stories

#### Format Specs
- **Content mix:** 50% value (tips/how-tos), 30% engagement (polls, questions), 20% connection (behind-the-scenes, personal)
- **Format:** Vertical, full-screen, ephemeral (24 hours)
- **Length:** Keep each Story frame to 1–3 seconds of reading time

#### Structural Template (Per Story Frame)
```
HEADLINE
[One bold statement or question]

3 BULLETS (optional)
[Key points, ultra-short]

1 CTA
[Poll, question sticker, "DM me", or swipe-up/link]
```

#### Formula Adaptation
Stories don't carry the full formula. Use one element per story: a failure moment, a quick lesson, a proof point, or a question. String multiple stories together for a mini-narrative arc across 3–5 frames.

## 6. Twitter/X

### Format Specs
- **Character limit:** 280 characters per tweet (threads for depth)
- **Best-performing format:** Text-only posts beat video and images for median engagement
- **Hashtags:** 0–2 max — more looks spammy
- **External links:** Use sparingly — reduces dwell time and dampens distribution
- **Posting cadence:** Multiple quality posts per day ideal; consistency critical
- **Pin:** Keep one high-value post or thread pinned for profile visitors

### Structural Template — Single Tweet
```
SHARP TAKE OR STAT (one sentence)
[Contrarian, specific, or result-based]

ACTIONABLE TIP (one sentence, optional)
[One thing they can do today]
```

### Structural Template — Thread
```
TWEET 1 — HOOK
[Bold claim, story opener, or result that demands the click]

TWEETS 2–6 — BODY (one idea per tweet)
[Story → lesson → action steps]
[Each tweet must stand alone AND flow in sequence]

TWEET 7 — RECAP + CTA
[Summary of key points]
[Follow, repost, or "bookmark this thread"]
```

### Formula Adaptation
Single tweets: pick ONE formula element — the failure, the lie, or the lesson. That's your entire tweet. Threads: use the full formula spread across tweets — Tweet 1 = failure/hook, Tweets 2–3 = lie + turn, Tweets 4–5 = lesson + proof, Tweet 6–7 = offer + CTA. Threads are the closest Twitter gets to the full 7-step.

### What Works
- Sharp opinions and strong takes
- Story → lesson → action structure for threads
- Text-only posts for reach
- Strategic replies on others' posts in your niche
- Mixing competence (expertise) with engagement (invite responses)

### What Doesn't Work
- Overusing hashtags or tagging many accounts
- Excess external links in every post
- Low-value or off-topic posts
- Long gaps between posting (account goes "cold")
- Generic comments on trending topics

## 7. Email Newsletter

### Format Specs
- **Goal per email:** ONE core goal (book a call, read an article, hit reply) — not ten
- **Structure:** Inverted pyramid — strongest value and main CTA at top, supporting details below
- **Tone:** Conversational but purposeful — not corporate, not casual
- **Subject line:** Clear value promise — this determines open rate
- **Length:** Long enough to deliver value, short enough to respect time — 500–1,000 words typical

### Structural Template — Educational Playbook Issue
```
SUBJECT LINE
[Clear benefit or curiosity gap — under 50 characters ideal]

OPENING HOOK (first 2–3 sentences)
[Story, stat, or bold claim that earns the read]

BODY — THE SYSTEM/FRAMEWORK (main section)
[Step-by-step breakdown, case study, or deep-dive lesson]
[Use subheads to break sections if over 500 words]

KEY TAKEAWAY (1–2 sentences)
[The one thing to remember or do this week]

CTA (single, clear)
[Reply, click, book, or download — ONE action]

P.S. (optional)
[Secondary CTA or personal note]
```

### Issue Types
- **Educational playbook:** Step-by-step systems, checklists, frameworks
- **Case study:** Restaurant transformation with numbers and before/after
- **Operator digest:** 3 curated links + 1 mindset insight + 1 metric of the week

### Formula Adaptation
The newsletter is the ONE format where the full 7-step formula fits completely — failure → lie → turn → lesson → proof → offer, with room to breathe. This is the authority-building format. LinkedIn compresses the formula; newsletters let it expand with story detail and depth.

### What Works
- Strong subject lines with clear value promise
- One message per email, one CTA
- Conversational tone with real stories
- Consistent cadence (weekly)
- P.S. line for secondary CTA

### What Doesn't Work
- Multiple competing CTAs
- Wall-of-text without structure
- Generic "content roundup" with no original insight
- Inconsistent sending schedule
- Selling in every email (see 90/10 rule — 90% value, 10% pitch)

## Adding New Platforms

When adding a new platform, document:
1. Character/length limits and sweet spot
2. Hook placement (where does the fold/cut happen?)
3. Structural template with labeled sections
4. Formula adaptation note (how much of the 7-step fits?)
5. What works / what doesn't on that platform
6. CTA style and link tolerance
