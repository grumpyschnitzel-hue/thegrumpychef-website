## Voice Checklist (Apply to Every Example)

Before any example is approved, it passes these tests:

- [ ] **Forest walk test:** Would Chris say this after a solo walk in the Black Forest?
- [ ] **60% capacity test:** Would an exhausted operator at 60% energy actually read this?
- [ ] **Dollar sign test:** Is there a specific number, metric, or outcome attached?

- [ ] **No guru tone:** Does it sound like a peer who's been there, not a coach selling a system?
- [ ] **One idea:** Is there exactly one core idea, not three?

## Example 9: Reddit Field Report — BURNOUT (Cross-platform)

**Awareness stage:** Problem Aware > Solution Aware
**Hook type:** Failure / Vulnerability (long form)
**CTA destination:** None (Reddit = no pitch. Profile stalking is the conversion.)

**Title:** I burned out twice running restaurants. Here's what I actually changed the second time.

**Body:**

First time: ran a restaurant in Australia for 3 years. 80-hour weeks. Lost my relationship. Lost 12kg. Told myself it was the cost of doing business.

I didn't change anything. I just moved countries and did it again.

Second time: running my own place in Germany. Same hours. Same chaos. Same "just until things stabilize." Except this time I was also $370K CAD in debt and watching my marriage crack.

What I changed (the boring version):

1. I wrote down every prep task. Every single one. Tied each to a par level and a sales forecast. Took a weekend. Boring. But suddenly any cook could prep without asking me 15 questions.

2. I stopped doing inventory from memory. Made a par sheet. Ordering went from "what do I think we need" to "what does the sheet say." Took 20 minutes to build. Cut food waste noticeably within 2 weeks.

3. I built a close-down checklist. Not a fancy one. A laminated piece of paper. Before that, I was the only person who could close properly. After, anyone could.

That's it. No software. No consultant. No "mindset shift." Three documents that meant I didn't have to be there for every decision.

My hours went from 80 to about 50 within a month. Then 45 over the next two months as the team got used to the systems.

I'm not going to pretend I'm fully "recovered." But I sleep. I see my family. And the restaurant runs the same whether I'm there or not.

If you're in the thick of it right now: you don't need motivation. You need to write things down. Start with the prep list.

Happy to share the templates if anyone wants them.

---

**Structural notes for SKILL.md:**
- Title: specific, honest, no clickbait
- Opening: two burnouts, not one. Shows the pattern.
- "What I changed": deliberately boring. Three documents. No magic.
- Tone: Reddit-native. No marketing language. No frameworks. Just "here's what I did."
- Close: offer to share, not a link. Let mods and community decide.

**Hook type:** Direct Challenge
**CTA destination:** Organic engagement

---

**Why this failed — structural diagnosis:**

1. **Carousels tank compared to text posts.** The same leadership topic got 3,202 (text) and 26,283 (text) impressions. As a carousel: 149. That's a 99% drop. LinkedIn's algorithm currently favors text posts heavily over document/carousel posts for accounts at Chris's growth stage.

2. **No scar in the caption.** The caption is generic: "3 things that changed everything." Compare to the onboarding post's opening: "I showed up to a new country for a new job. Nobody was in the kitchen." The carousel caption gives no reason to swipe.

3. **"Swipe through" is a weak CTA.** It asks for effort without earning it. The best text posts pull you in — carousels have to sell the swipe in the first 3 seconds.

4. **No alt text on slides.** LinkedIn can't index the content for search or feed distribution. The algorithm literally can't read what's in the carousel.

5. **The topic already performed as text.** Same leadership angle, same audience. The audience already engaged with the text version. A carousel rehash adds format complexity without adding value.

**Rules to add from this data:**
- At Chris's current growth stage, text posts outperform carousels by 20-100x
- Do NOT carousel a topic that already worked as text — it cannibalizes without adding
- If using carousels: the caption must contain the scar/hook (not just "swipe through")
- Always add alt text to carousel slides for algorithm indexing
- Carousels may work better at higher follower counts with established engagement patterns

**Use this example when:** Someone suggests a carousel. Show this data first. Text is the winning format right now.
