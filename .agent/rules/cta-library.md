# CTA Library: By Platform, Goal & Awareness Stage

> **Purpose:** Ready-to-use CTAs organized by goal, platform format, and Schwartz awareness stage. Every cell is a plug-and-play CTA for content production.
> **Not in this file:** Writing formula, voice rules, platform format specs, hooks. Those live in their own files.
> **Rule:** SKILL.md selects the goal + awareness stage + platform, then pulls the matching CTA from this file. One CTA per post. No stacking.

---

## Core Principles

**Trust before conversion.** The best marketing feels like help. If the content doesn't improve their day — make them feel seen, make a problem feel smaller, make a solution feel doable — it is asking for attention instead of deserving it. Never select a CTA that sacrifices trust for conversion. The audience can feel the pitch coming. (See: "The Governing Principle" in platform-templates.md.)

**One CTA per post. One.** Multiple asks trigger decision paralysis. Every post gets one primary CTA matched to its goal and awareness stage.

**CTA formula:** Verb + Step + Reason. "Comment GUIDE below and I'll DM you the checklist" beats "Check out my link" because it explains the next step and the payoff.

## Platform Fitness Matrix

Not every CTA belongs on every platform at every stage. This matrix shows where each awareness stage naturally fits. Use it before selecting a CTA from the goal tables below.

**Legend:**
- ✅ = Recommended (default path — use freely)
- ⚡ = Use sparingly (works in specific contexts — retargeting, warm reply threads, personal DMs)
- ❌ = Not recommended (mismatched intent — will feel pushy, get flagged, or get ignored)

| Stage | LinkedIn Text | LinkedIn Carousel | Substack Notes | FB Groups | IG Reels | IG Carousel | IG Stories | Twitter/X | Email Newsletter |
|-------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Unaware** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ⚡ |
| **Problem Aware** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| **Solution Aware** | ✅ | ✅ | ⚡ | ⚡ | ⚡ | ⚡ | ⚡ | ✅ | ✅ |
| **Product Aware** | ⚡ | ⚡ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ⚡ | ⚡ | ✅ |
| **Most Aware** | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

**Why this pattern:**
- Social platforms are top-of-funnel. Unaware and Problem Aware CTAs (questions, curiosity, DM keywords) live here.
- Email is the one channel that spans all 5 stages because they opted in. Permission was earned.
- Solution Aware is the bridge — works on authority platforms (LinkedIn, X threads) but feels pushy in casual spaces (Substack Notes, FB groups, IG).
- Product/Most Aware CTAs (book a call, buy now, reply READY) belong in email, DMs, or warm retargeting — never cold social.

### Platform-Specific "Message Me If..." Rules

- **LinkedIn:** Use DM keywords (KITCHEN, MATRIX, DISCOVERY). Specific keywords > "message me."
- **Instagram:** Evolve into "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll DM you" to leverage algorithm signals AND start DM conversations simultaneously. Set up ManyChat when ready.
- **Facebook Groups:** Always offer DM after delivering value in the post. Never lead with the DM ask.
- **Substack Notes:** "Reply" not "DM" — Notes are conversation-first. DM asks feel transactional here.
- **Twitter/X:** Works as-is but add a qualifier: "DM me if you're working through [specific problem]" — filters tire-kickers.
- **YouTube:** Transform into "Comment [KEYWORD] below" or "Link in description" — DMs don't exist on YouTube.
- **Email:** "Reply" is the DM equivalent. "Reply READY" or "Reply DISCOVERY" — same keyword psychology, different channel.
