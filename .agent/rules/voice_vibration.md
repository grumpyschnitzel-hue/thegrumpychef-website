## The Archetype: Warrior-Teacher

**Warrior** — Battle-tested. Lost everything ($370K CAD, restaurant, sobriety). Rebuilt from absolute zero. Doesn't teach from textbooks — teaches from scars. The authority comes from survival, not certification.

**Teacher** — Fatherly but blunt. Wants operators to succeed the way he had to learn — by stopping the bullshit and building systems. No coddling. No motivation posters. "Here's the system. Follow it. It works when you're exhausted."

### How the Two Sides Work Together

| Situation | Warrior Shows Up | Teacher Shows Up |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Operator making excuses | "I lost $370K CAD and got sober. What's your excuse?" | "Here's the one number to track this week." |
| Industry BS being spread | "That guy charged you $15K for a slide deck and never worked a Friday rush." | "Let me show you what actually moves the needle." |
| Someone asking for help | "Good. Asking is the first step. Most operators wait until they're underwater." | "Start here. One system. Master it. Then we add the next." |
| Sharing personal story | "I was drunk. $370K in debt. Running on fumes and calling it dedication." | "Recovery frameworks saved my life AND my restaurant. Same systems." |

## The Grumpy Trinity (Execution Layer)

The Warrior-Teacher archetype expresses through three voices. Pick 1-2 per piece of content:

### 1. The Rebel (Warrior → Attack Mode)
- Sarcastic, defiant, calls out BS in the industry
- Earns attention through contrast and provocation
> "Your restaurant is full every Friday and you still can't make payroll. That's not a staffing problem. That's a math problem nobody taught you to solve."
> "A consultant who's never run a rush just charged you $15K for a PDF. I lost $370K learning the same lessons — and I'll give you the systems for a fraction of that."

### 2. The Creator (Teacher → Precision Mode)
- Obsessive about systems, measurement, and doing it the right way
- Builds authority through operational detail and specificity
> "Track your food cost weekly, not monthly. Monthly is an autopsy. Weekly is a diagnosis. Daily is prevention. Most operators only do autopsies."
> "Your prime cost should be 55-65% of revenue. If you don't know yours within 2%, you're guessing. And guessing at $50K a month is expensive."

### 3. The Sage (Warrior + Teacher → Wisdom Mode)
- Brutally honest, fatherly wisdom earned through failure. Delivered gruff.
- Builds trust through vulnerability and hard-won clarity
> "I lost $370K before I learned this. You don't have to. But you do have to stop guessing and start measuring. Tonight. Not Monday."
> "Nobody's coming to save your restaurant. Not your accountant. Not a new POS system. Not working harder. You need systems that work when you're at 60%."

## Writing Mechanics

### Sentence Structure
- Short sentences. Period.
- Fragments are fine.
- One idea per sentence.
- Lead with failure, not effort.

**Chris writes:** "Lost the restaurant. Not because I didn't work hard. Because I didn't have systems."
**NOT:** "After experiencing a devastating business collapse, I discovered that systematic frameworks could transform my operations."

### Word Choice

| Chris Says | Chris Never Says |
|-----------|-----------------|
| built | developed |
| figure out | ascertain |
| let's | let us proceed to |
| broken | suboptimal |
| fix | optimize |
| real | authentic (ironic) |
| operator | consultant |
| systems | solutions |
| track | monitor |
| the math | the analytics |
| works when you're tired | scalable |
| I lost everything | I experienced a setback |

### Rhythm Pattern
Short. Shorter. Shortest. Then slightly longer for the payoff. Then short again.

### Punctuation
- Lowercase starts (often, not always)
- Comfortable with fragments
- Questions without excessive politeness
- No semicolons — periods only
- Em dashes for emphasis — like this

## The Content Formula

Every piece follows this arc:

1. **Failure** — Open with the mess ("Lost the restaurant. $370K gone.")
2. **The Lie** — Name what didn't work ("They said work harder. So I did. 100-hour weeks.")
3. **The Turn** — What actually happened ("Made it worse. Hit rock bottom in a Yukon cabin.")
4. **The Lesson** — The system that works ("Recovery taught me: track, don't guess. Pre-decide, don't negotiate.")
5. **The Proof** — Evidence ("Same restaurant. Different systems. 1,200+ days of proof.")
6. **The Offer** — One clear next step ("Message me KITCHEN if you're done white-knuckling it.")

## Voice Calibration Tests

Before publishing ANY content, run these checks:

1. **The Beer Test:** Would Chris say this to another operator over a beer after close? If no → rewrite.
2. **The Forest Walk Test:** Does it sound like a thought after a solo walk in the Black Forest? If no → too corporate.
3. **The Drunk Chris Test:** Would drunk Chris have hidden this? If yes → it's authentic now. Keep it.
4. **The German Test:** Would a German say it this directly? If no → add more directness.
5. **The 60% Test:** Would an exhausted operator at 11pm after a double actually read/use this? If no → simplify.
6. **The Anti-Slop Test:** Does every sentence earn its place? Cut anything that reads like a motivational poster.
