# Hook Library: By Type, Pain Point & Awareness Stage

> **Purpose:** Ready-to-use opening lines for content production. Every cell is a plug-and-play hook mapped to a specific pain point and awareness stage.
> **Not in this file:** CTAs, platform format specs, writing formula, voice rules. Those live in their own files.
> **Rule:** SKILL.md selects the pain point + awareness stage + hook type, then pulls the matching hook from this file. The hook is always lines 1–3 of the content. Everything else follows.

---

## Core Hook Principles

1. **Create an information gap.** Hint at value but withhold the "how" or "why." Curiosity pulls them past the fold.
2. **Lead with specificity.** Numbers, timeframes, concrete situations. "18 months" beats "a while." "$2,400/month" beats "significant savings."
3. **Start with their pain, not your offer.** The first line is about their problem, never your credentials or product.
4. **Use pattern interrupts.** Say something unexpected, contrarian, or uncomfortably honest to break the scroll.
5. **Keep hooks short.** 1–2 lines max on Reels/Shorts. Trim every filler word. No "today I want to talk about…"
6. **Voice test:** Would Chris say this after a solo walk in the Black Forest? If it sounds like a marketing template, rewrite it.

## The 8 Hook Types

| # | Type | What It Does | Template Pattern |
|---|------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Failure / Vulnerability | Opens with your mess, builds trust through honesty | "I did [thing] and still [bad outcome]. Here's what I missed." |
| 2 | Contrarian Take | Challenges accepted wisdom, triggers disagreement | "Everyone says [common advice]. It's killing your [result]." |
| 3 | Stat-Driven | Leads with a surprising number to stop the scroll | "[Specific stat]. Here's what that means for your restaurant." |
| 4 | Question | Opens a loop the reader needs closed | "What if [desired outcome] without [feared cost]?" |
| 5 | Direct Challenge | Calls out a behavior, creates productive discomfort | "If you still [bad habit], you're leaving [result] on the table." |
| 6 | "Nobody Tells You…" | Promises insider knowledge, triggers FOMO | "Here's what nobody tells you about [topic]." |
| 7 | Result / Proof | Leads with a specific outcome, builds credibility | "I [action] and [measurable result] in [timeframe]." |
| 8 | Pattern Interrupt | Says something weird, unexpected, or jarring to break the scroll | "[Unexpected statement that doesn't sound like marketing]." |
