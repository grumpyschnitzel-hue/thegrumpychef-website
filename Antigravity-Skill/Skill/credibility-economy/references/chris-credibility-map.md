## The Big Scars (Lead-with-these)

### 1. The $370K CAD Collapse
- **What happened:** Lost the restaurant, racked up $370K in debt
- **The lie that failed:** "Work harder. More hours. Grind through it."
- **The lesson:** Systems beat effort. You can't outwork bad math.
- **Credibility asset:** Proves he's not teaching theory — he paid $370K for this education
- **Use when:** Talking about financial systems, prime cost, the cost of guessing

### 2. Getting Sober / 1,200+ Days
- **What happened:** Hit rock bottom. Got sober in a Yukon cabin.
- **The lie that failed:** "I can manage it. I just need to cut back."
- **The lesson:** Recovery frameworks work for business too. Pre-decide. No exceptions. Track, don't guess.
- **Credibility asset:** Nobody else in restaurant consulting bridges recovery to operations
- **Use when:** Talking about discipline systems, pre-deciding, eliminating negotiation, consistency

### 3. The Yukon Factor
- **What happened:** Ran a restaurant in Dawson City (population 1,300, -40C winters)
- **The lie that failed:** "You need a big market to succeed."
- **The lesson:** Constraints force systems. When you can't get a delivery for 3 days, your par levels better be right.
- **Credibility asset:** If it works in the Yukon, it works anywhere
- **Use when:** Talking about par levels, ordering systems, constraint-based thinking, extreme operations

## How to Use This Map

When creating any content:
1. Identify the topic
2. Find the matching scar(s) above
3. Lead with the scar, then deliver the system
4. Include the dollar figure or measurable outcome
5. End with the tool/system that prevents the scar from happening to the reader

**The formula:** Scar + System + Dollar Figure = Credible Content

Example: "I was reviewing food cost monthly. By the time I saw the problem, I'd already lost $4,200. Now I track weekly — takes 20 minutes. The Weekly Prime Cost Tracker catches drift in 7 days, not 30. Here's the exact spreadsheet."
